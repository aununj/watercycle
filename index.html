Evaporation

The sun heats up water in oceans, lakes and rivers. The heat energy turns liquid water into water vapor — an invisible gas — that rises into the sky.

🤓 Fun fact: Even puddles and wet laundry evaporate! Plants release water vapor too, in a process called transpiration.

    Skill 4 · Abstraction

    Hide the details, keep the big idea

    The real water cycle is way more complex than 4 icons — but scientists (and programmers!) simplify it into a model so it's easier to understand and remember.

    In reality, evaporation speed depends on temperature, wind, humidity, and how much surface water is exposed to air. Water leaves oceans, lakes, rivers, damp soil, and even living things through transpiration and respiration. Clouds form at many different altitudes and temperatures, made of liquid droplets or ice crystals depending on conditions. Precipitation can fall as rain, snow, sleet, freezing rain, or hail, depending on the temperature of the air it passes through. Some water soaks deep underground into aquifers, some runs off into streams, and some is taken up by plant roots before ever reaching a river. It's a messy, tangled, constantly-moving system!
